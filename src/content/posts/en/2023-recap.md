# Preface

This year was probably the one that changed me the most and brought the most into my life so far. Starting in 2024, I want to make it a habit to record what I saw and experienced in the year before, so that the people, events, and moments that changed me, helped me, or left unforgettable memories can stay with my future self.

# Record

I noticed that a lot of my classmates had a habit of writing yearly recaps, so I decided to do the same.
I’ll organize everything month by month.

## January

### Mock Exams

A new year had arrived, and it was also the year I was preparing for university. At the time, I was in the second semester of my final year at vocational high school, right in the last sprint before the standardized exam and still grinding through mock exams. Ever since I had prepared for the written test of the Industrial Technology Competition while also studying for mock exams, and ended up placing 18th nationwide, my grades had been dropping in a straight line. I think part of the reason was that, deep down, I believed my time should be spent improving my professional skills rather than preparing for dull entrance exams. So I split my time 70/30, **70% on improving myself and 30% on exam prep. After getting into university, reality proved that I was right.**

### Wanting to Become a Tutor

So why did I still want to prepare for the exam? Even teachers in my department said I didn’t really need to anymore. A big reason was that I wanted to start private tutoring during summer vacation, and for tutoring, I still needed some decent entrance exam results to show.

Actually, when I was in elementary and junior high school, my dream was to become a teacher. I really liked the feeling of teaching and helping students grow. But along the way I saw way too many ridiculous kids doing ridiculous things, and it gradually talked me out of wanting to be a school teacher XD. So I figured, why not just become a tutor instead? Earn some extra money, teach a bit, and enjoy life.

### Percentile Ranking for the Recommendation Program Released!

After struggling through two and a half years, the results of my efforts finally showed.

Back then, I had endured memorizing question banks, P.E., military training content, while also constantly complaining about the absurdity of the education system. I pushed through an environment with weak competitiveness and scarce educational resources, gritting my teeth until the deadline for school-grade evaluation in the recommendation program.

After the results came out, six internal academic indicators were counted, including total grades, internship scores, professional subjects, Chinese, English, math, and so on. I ranked in the **top 1% of my school** in all of them. It was not exactly surprising, but seeing it still made me breathe a sigh of relief, because once those percentages came out, if they were all 1%, then getting into NTUST was basically guaranteed. At that point, I already had one foot through the gate and could practically lie down and get in.

But honestly, I wasn’t satisfied with just that. I wanted more than simply getting into NTUST. I wanted to be the overall nationwide top-ranked student across all categories. At the time, my seventh tie-breaker score (competitions, certifications, language tests) was 72 points. I had one gold medal in the Industrial Technology Competition, a GEPT Intermediate-High listening and reading certificate, four Class C licenses, and two Class B licenses. You could say I had maxed it out. I genuinely believed no one could score higher than me, because just keeping up with schoolwork was already a huge pressure. To also win national competitions on top of that was extremely rare. So I spent those months waiting for the May announcement, expecting to see myself ranked first overall XD. But... as you’ll see later, that didn’t happen. Let’s set that aside for now.

## February, March, April

During this stretch, I spent a lot of time exploring my interests. I was mainly doing two things: first, learning data structures and algorithms; second, deciding whether I wanted to study Computer Science or Electronics at NTUST XD. I even went to the Electronics department to borrow an FPGA board and played around with Verilog, and I asked many people for their opinions. In the end, I still chose to move toward computer science.

I also followed along with my class on practice books, but honestly my heart wasn’t really in it. My mock exam ranking floated around 50th to 100th nationwide. My final wish was simply to score around 610 overall and stay within the top 100 nationwide.

In April, I also received a notice from the New Taipei City Department of Education about an overseas vocational study tour. The day after finishing the exam, we would be flying to Australia for two weeks!

The first time in my life that I went abroad was actually because I won an award XD. Being taken overseas by the Department of Education really was... really nice.

## May

### Trip to Australia

I finished the exam! Time to go abroad. For the details, please read this post:

-> [New Taipei City Overseas Vocational Study Tour: A Trip to Australia](https://viiccwen.github.io/NewTaipeiCity-Australia-Records/) <-

During those two weeks, the twelve of us attended classes together every day, ate together, slept together (well, maybe not that), and chatted a lot. I was really happy to meet them all. I learned a lot in Australia and made many wonderful memories.

### Recommendation Program Results!

This was one of the few regrets I had that year, though I don’t think it was because I didn’t work hard enough. The whole thing was just absurd....

When the ranking list was released, I was 1st out of 505 in the Electrical and Electronics group, meaning I was the top student in that category. But in the overall ranking across all groups, I was only fourth! That was already shocking enough, and it got even crazier after that: **the top three all came from the same school?**

![Recommendation ranking](https://imgur.com/zkPgpIs.jpg)

Later I found out that those three students had taken a huge number of certificates from other categories just to compete for the recommendation title, and those certificates pushed my ranking down. The first-place student had apparently taken more than twenty certificates?＿？

I could only say... what kind of bizarre move was that? But later I let it go, because normal people do not do this kind of thing. It’s really only private schools chasing leaderboard rankings that would pull off something so meaningless. I mean, isn’t it weird to make a student from Learning Resources go take certifications in prepress processes and pastry making?

Still, being the top student in the Electrical and Electronics group was enough to satisfy me, and it also gave me some confidence, the confidence to walk into NTUST and compete with all the other monsters there.

## June

### Trip to Germany

A few months earlier, I had also received a notice from the Ministry of Education. They were preparing to take the first- and second-place Industrial Technology Competition students on a two-week vocational visit to Germany.

That’s right. I got to go abroad again! (spins excitedly)

But I’ve been too busy, so I still haven’t paid off my “Germany trip write-up debt” hahaha. Once I finally clear that debt, I’ll update the link. Friends from the Germany group, please wait for me a little longer!

On that trip, I got to meet even more amazing people. Since everyone there was either the champion or runner-up in their category, they were all top vocational students. Even after entering NTUST, I still ran into familiar faces quite often XD.

## July, August

### Tutoring

Since I met my own expectations on the entrance exam and ranked within the top 100 nationwide, with results that were fairly solid overall (except math, which exploded QQ), I started looking for students in tutoring groups~.

As the saying goes, “everything is hard at the beginning.” I really felt that at the time. **Everyone in those tutoring groups came from elite schools like Jianguo, Taipei First Girls, or Affiliated High School**, and I was the one NTUST student standing out in the middle. More than once I started doubting my life choices and asking myself, **can I really find any students like this?**

But I didn’t give up. I posted once every week, and after fifteen painful days of waiting, I was lucky enough to find two students in the end! I was also really happy they were both in my hometown, so I didn’t have to go all the way to Taipei to teach, which would have cost both energy and money.

It was also around this time that I really felt the value of communication skills. Since I had been active in student council back in junior high school and often interacted with adults, I had an advantage in speaking and expressing myself. Not long after I started tutoring, I got the hang of it smoothly. I got along well with both students and parents, and I also managed to save some money for myself.

## September, October, November, December

The next four months were incredibly fulfilling. Also exhausting....

### Peers

A vocational high school kid was about to step into his dream school, and everything felt so wonderful, especially the cafeteria XD. At the freshman tea gathering, I met many classmates who were seriously talented, which made me look forward to the future even more. We would grow together and compete together.

And I even got elected vice class representative) x.

### Dorm

First of all, if you’re a male freshman, then obviously you’re not going to get the nicest dorm, right?

So I moved into a six-person room in Dorm 2. Our room had four Computer Science students and two Architecture students.

When we first moved in, everyone was super quiet XD. It was just me, Boying, and Siyuan, the two big shots, talking. But everyone turned out to be great roommates and never did anything too outrageous. Honestly, I even felt like I was the loudest person in the room ==. Sometimes I made too much noise and felt like I was bothering people, but I really didn’t mean to QQ.

I’m also really glad I lived in the dorm. On every late quiet night, there was always someone fighting alongside me, even if that sounds a little tragic....

### Clubs

#### Dance Club

Before entering university, I already thought to myself that I definitely had to join a dance club....

So I did.

But I only treated dancing as an interest and a form of exercise, so I didn’t spend too much time on it, which meant I missed a lot of activities. But that’s life, you have to give something up to gain something else QQ.

#### NTUST GDSC

I also joined NTUST GDSC (Google Developer Student Clubs), the Open Source Research Club. There I got to learn some Google tools and also met many talented people from different fields through the club. It was basically a stepping stone that broadened both my horizons and my network.

What I found a bit unfortunate, though, was that NTUST GDSC didn’t seem to put that much effort into club operations, and there weren’t even many chances to do project development. So in the future I might join GDSC events at other schools, or once I take over a leadership role later, I can create some project opportunities myself. That’s part of my future plan.

I even joined an exchange event: [2023 GDSC Northern District 13-School Joint Meetup](https://viiccwen.github.io/GDSC-NorthDistrict-13-Interaction/)

### Tutoring

After entering university, I was still gritting my teeth and holding on. I even took on one more student, which meant I had to travel back and forth on weekends. Because of that, I missed many department and club activities, which was a bit of a pity QQ.

But after moving into my second year, I’ll probably stop the tutoring case in Keelung. Otherwise my body and mind are really going to suffer badly.

Later I also realized that the exhausting part was not teaching. It was commuting. On Fridays, just getting back to Keelung could take two and a half hours because of traffic. It was miserable and tiring enough to drive anyone insane.

Still, I think having some extra income in college is important. It makes life feel fuller, right)?

### Teaching Assistant

I once saw a professor recruiting teaching assistants on the NTUST forum. As just a little freshman, I gathered up my courage and submitted my resume XD. It was honestly crazy.

On the first day of school, I even went to T4 for the interview. At the time, I didn’t even know that the first digit of a classroom number represented the floor XD. I talked a lot with that professor from the Department of Applied Foreign Languages. Although I didn’t get that position in the end, which I never really expected anyway, the professor said I left a very deep impression and recommended me to another professor, which is how my freshman-year TA life began....

And honestly, I personally think the second professor’s position was even better. First of all, it was remote, which already beats 99% of on-campus student jobs for someone as busy as me. Second, it was easy. At most I just entered grades and recorded scores on Moodle. Other than that, there was not much to do. I could just wait to get paid each month. Amazing!

I absolutely do not regret sending out that resume back then.

### Coursework

#### Calculus

I was honestly so scared that my calculus would turn into “crisis-lus” ==, because my math really isn’t that strong. So I had no choice but to be a good student: do my homework, submit the bonus assignments, and collect every extra point I could.

At midterm, my mindset almost cracked because I only got 91/120, and everyone around me had scores higher than mine. My face froze, and I realized: **I’m actually not that amazing.**

But by the time finals came, I had gotten too immersed in coding ==. I had basically made no progress in calculus and only realized with one week left: “I’m doomed....”

So after a week of hard catching up, I went from Chapter 6 all the way to the end of Chapter 7 and got a pretty decent score, 89. I think the highest score was only around 96. In the end, I finished Calculus I with an A+ and a full score overall~ Looking forward to how Calculus II goes XD.

#### Introduction to Computer Science

I honestly had no idea what this class was doing.... I heard the professor talk about Fourier series, Laplace transforms, JPEG encoding, U.S. university rankings, and whether Zuckerberg the lizard person can speak Chinese.

The most painful part was when he said, “Anything I’ve mentioned can appear on the exam~” I nearly broke down inside. I spent even more time preparing for this midterm than for calculus, because there were barely any multiple-choice questions, almost all short-answer questions.

In the end I got 99/110.... What I remember most was the score distribution chart. There were 102 students in total, and nearly forty of them got above 90? And I was only ranked fifteenth??

![NTUST Intro to CS ranking](https://imgur.com/JlRbXFx.jpg)

That hit my ego on an entirely new level.

#### General Education Courses

They were basically a pile of report-writing and presentation classes. The number of words I wrote this semester (7000 + 6000 + 3000 + 1200 + 2500) was probably more than everything I had written in the previous 18 years combined ==. I can say with confidence that the time I spent on reports could absolutely have been used to build a whole project ==.

But I do think the general education courses I chose actually taught me something, and my teammates were all pretty carrying. In the end, I also finished them all with A+ grades~ truly worth celebrating!

### Certifications

#### CPE

Because the programming professor said the CPE could add points to our grade, and **one solved problem was worth five extra points**, how could we possibly miss a chance like that to farm free points? Of course we had to go all in.

So I took it twice, solved 4/7 problems both times, and got 20 extra points. My final course grade went straight into the 110+ range. The professor even called out students like us in class for having no sense of martial ethics XD, and told us not to come to the final exam anymore (though I still went hahaha).

I was actually pretty surprised, because I thought I’d probably only solve two or three problems. Instead I solved four ==. The first time I placed in the top 2.3%, and the second time I got into the top 0.9%, which basically means I was PR99 for that session... insane.

#### GEPT Intermediate-High Speaking and Writing

I’m very sure this one won’t pass XD. I’m too weak. I even started thinking maybe I shouldn’t take it again, because it’s only really useful in Taiwan. I’d be better off preparing for TOEIC, TOEFL, or IELTS instead....

### Competitions

#### NCPC

All I can say is that I was still too inexperienced back then. I was too weak, and I also needed to choose teammates more carefully....

I already have some suitable people in mind, so I’ll probably go again. At the very least, I want to make it to ICPC ==.

#### ITSA

bubu and I teamed up for this one, but this competition was really strange. The preliminary round actually allowed internet access)?

Naturally, we made it to the finals.

Since the finals were in Tainan, we even went to ask a professor, our sponsor, whether he could help cover our transportation and accommodation. Otherwise I really would not have wanted to go compete....

Thankfully, Professor Wenkai was incredibly kind and willing to support us. I was honestly so touched QQ.

While preparing, we estimated the difficulty based on rankings from previous years and thought getting top three should be easy. Unexpectedly, it got harder every year ==. We only ended up fifth, though honestly I was the one dragging us down.... I offer my solemn apology to bubu.

![A photo of me and National Cheng Kung University](https://imgur.com/3czi6ln.jpg)

Still, we each got 2500 in prize money at least.

In the end, this also became the stepping stone that started my path into web development!

### Food

The food at NTUST is seriously amazing. There are three dining areas with around 20 to 45 vendors in total. The buffet lets you get full for very little money, the hot pot and gyudon are cheap and delicious, and there are even healthy meal boxes. If you want good fried chicken, pork cutlets, or ramen, Gongguan has all of that too, and compared with other parts of Taipei, it’s super affordable ==. For everyone’s sake, please go check Instagram for the food details hahaha.

# Conclusion

This year was truly colorful and eventful. It wasn’t just about changes in my mindset. I also met many highly capable peers who could grow and improve alongside me, and I had the chance to study abroad. All of these were incredibly rare experiences. I hope each coming year will be even more exciting than the last!
