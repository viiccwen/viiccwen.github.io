# Preface

I was really happy to attend the 17-school GDSC Joint Technical Exchange Event on 2024/04/21 (Sun). My feelings were similar to last time. I was not only hoping to exchange ideas about technology and projects, but also to meet more people who are shining in different fields, so that I would not feel alone on my own path in life and could stay motivated to keep moving forward.

But this time, my role was different. I had gone from being a General Member to becoming a **Core Team Member**.

# Event Schedule

# Icebreaker

Everyone had to stand up and introduce themselves, but also incorporate the role they drew XD. For example, our group got "teacher."

Then I ran off to chat with people from other groups that I did not know. I met two upperclassmen from NTHU and NYCU, and I kept asking them a lot about future directions. Even though they kept telling me not to rush hahaha, having no direction for the future really can make you anxious. I am still searching for the direction I want to take, and I do not want to let myself stay stuck in place.

# Project Sharing from Each School

## NTNU Jiubit

The sharing session that left the deepest impression on me was definitely from the NTNU students!

They introduced a project called "NTNU Jiubit." By filling out a Google Form, the system would recommend the most suitable match for you.

But their social media editor had it pretty rough. They were actually sending emails manually... more than 400 of them. Even just hearing about it made me feel like collapsing. Why not automate it XD.

The funniest part was that someone kept submitting a bunch of records, so the organizers had to send that person an email asking them to stop XD.

Later, we also chatted with them a bit and thought that maybe this idea could be adapted for NTUST too, and make an NTUST Jiubit.

# Game

The Kahoot segment!

Sadly, my GDSC power level was not high enough, and I ended up in fourth place. I cried. (But I did manage to bargain for a small gift hehe.)

# Free Networking

Compared with last time, I felt much more familiar with this kind of setting. Maybe last time I was fighting alone, going to the exchange event by myself to meet other people, so I seemed a bit timid. But this time I walked right up and chatted with everyone naturally. I also met an amazing senior from Taipei Medical University, who was a GitHub Campus Ambassador. Absolutely insane. Orz, seriously too strong.

I also talked with some of the seniors about future plans. One of them is going abroad for graduate school, which is actually something I have thought about too. But reality really hits hard QQ, and part of it is also the financial side.

This event was shorter than the last one, though, so it felt like everyone had not talked enough yet. So a bunch of us decided to go get dinner together afterward.

# Dinner Gathering of 11 People

Later, everyone grouped up and went to eat. Four future NTUST GDSC Core Team Members led seven newly met friends to go have barbecue together!

We went to [99% Yakiniku](https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100092295227119), on Leye Street in Da'an District.

During the gathering, some people talked about anime, some talked about university, some talked about clubs, hobbies, projects, and more. I was really happy that in just half a day, I could gain new friendships and even chat so naturally without any barriers. We ended up eating all the way until after nine o'clock.

And we ate so much XD. It was not even 200 NTD per person, which was amazing.

# Closing Thoughts

It feels like every exchange event happens around midterms, so people never get to talk that much XD. And unlike last time, the people were all different this time, all new faces.

This was my second time attending a GDSC exchange event. I am really grateful that the Lead from Taipei Medical University keeps organizing these exchange events so everyone can share technology and learn about what other schools are doing. It is a bit of a pity that NTUST was not one of the co-organizers again, though. I hope that after I am onboarded, we can become a co-organizer too, hehe. I also hope that once I officially take on the role, I can lead NTUST GDSC well in exploring technology and running the club properly, so that everyone can feel like one big family.

As always, I hope that in the future I can also become someone who shines brightly on stage and stands out across different fields. Let this post be an encouragement to myself.
