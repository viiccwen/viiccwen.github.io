# Preface

On 2023/11/12 (Sun), after overcoming a lot just to make it there, I attended the GDSC Northern District 13-School Joint Exchange Event. I originally went in with the mindset of wanting to talk to people and meet more individuals who are shining in different fields, hoping they could inspire me to keep going further on this path.

Was the event worth it? All I can say is that this was probably **one of the decisions I was most grateful to myself for making this year**.

# Event Schedule

10:00-10:15 Check-in

10:15-11:30 Each school's GDSC shared their club plans

11:30-11:40 Firebase Auth (Instructor Li Zhi-Wei)

11:40-12:00 Icebreaker game

12:00-13:00 Lunch

13:00-14:00 Introduction to GDG (Instructor Kevin Chiu)

14:00-16:00 Dialogflow x LINEbot Workshop (Instructor Kevin Chiu)

16:00-17:00 Free networking

# Each School's GDSC Sharing

I originally thought NTUST GDSC already had a pretty packed schedule of activities, but it turned out there is always someone even more intense. Some of the schools, like NTNU, NCCU, and Tamkang, had so many project development results and competition experiences that it honestly felt a bit overwhelming. You could clearly see how much emphasis they placed on club participation and technical depth. Seeing that whole lineup of projects gave me something to aspire to and also gave me a goal for myself: I hope that **during winter break I can find people to develop projects with and also complete my own portfolio project independently**, so I can add some highlights to my background.

# Firebase Auth (Instructor Li Zhi-Wei)

This big-name guest absolutely deserves to be called legendary... During his university years, he founded [INSIDE](https://www.inside.com.tw/) and [iCook](https://icook.tw/), and now he is the CEO of [The News Lens](https://www.thenewslens.com/). I have even used articles from their site in my presentations before.

Honestly, you could tell from his speaking ability and the way he thinks why he became successful. Although most of the session was about Firebase, he also mixed in stories about entrepreneurship, the difficulties he encountered along the way, and how he dealt with them. So it was not just a chance to learn knowledge, but also to hear real startup experience. It was seriously top-tier.

# Icebreaker Game

We played Switch games, and I took the chance to get to know the students sitting next to me. One of them was even a senior from our own school.

There was also an NTNU computer science student who was really good at whistling, and he left a strong impression on me hahaha. He said he graduated from Jianguo High School. Truly a god-tier person. He also had a very strong sense of being himself XD, kind of like an artist.

# Introduction to GDG & Dialogflow x LINEbot Workshop

Honestly, I feel like when we later went out to eat, the explanation of GDG was actually even clearer than the session itself XD. Anyway, it was basically introducing GDG and casually promoting [DevFest Taipei 2023](https://gdg.community.dev/events/details/google-gdg-taipei-presents-devfest-taipei-2023/) hahaha.

After that, we learned how to build a LINE Bot with "zero coding." I had actually built a LINE Bot myself in Python before, but I still think Dialogflow is a pretty good tool. Compared with coding everything yourself, it can save a lot of time, though its extensibility is definitely weaker.

![](https://imgur.com/Cwtrw9r.jpg)

We also got to do a real round of "**Agile software development**" XD. Five people spent one hour building a LINE Bot together, each person taking responsibility for a different part, figuring out who would handle which section and who would handle the LINE Bot itself. In the end, we managed to finish it, and we almost failed to make it in time hahaha. (But I even made a Slides deck for it, which elevated the whole thing.)

![](https://imgur.com/gEBKENv.jpg)

My feeling after it ended: so this was actually a speedrun... I thought I had come to the GDSC Northern District 13-School Joint Exchange Event.

# Free Networking & Being Amazed

At this event I really worked up a lot of courage. After all, everyone else was senior to me, and we were only meeting by chance. But I still went over and started awkward conversations, whether to ask for advice on how I should develop in the future or because I hoped to eventually take on an **NTUST GDSC Core Team/Lead** role. The valuable experience and suggestions from the seniors taught me so much. I am seriously incredibly grateful to them.

At the same time, I really admired the Leads from Taipei Medical University and Tamkang. Quietly saying this, they are my idols right now. I also want to become someone extraordinary.

In the end, I even shamelessly asked if I could go eat dinner with them XD.

# Dinner Gathering with a Leader and Two Little Members

We went to A13 to eat hot pot, and it was such a long walk.

On the way there, I talked quite a bit with Instructor Kevin. He is a really NICE teacher. He listened to my questions and also gave me some ideas about career paths. I am really thankful to him, and I hope we will still have chances to meet in the future.

I also met a senior who can really eat XD. She could finish six plates of meat by herself, which was honestly unbelievable. I just watched those six plates gradually disappear. She is such a small person too, and I seriously do not know how she managed to eat that much...

# Closing Thoughts

In the end, all I want to say is that I am really glad I came to this event. It gave me a lot of motivation to keep striving forward. It was not just simple networking. I met many important people, broadened my horizons, and those valuable connections and experiences are things money could never compare to.

I also hope that in the future I can become someone like them, someone who shines brightly on stage and stands out across different fields. Let this post be an encouragement to myself.
