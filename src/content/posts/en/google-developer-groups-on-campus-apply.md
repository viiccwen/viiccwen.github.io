When we talk about campus tech clubs, one of the most well-known is definitely Google Developer Student Clubs, the former name of Google Developer Groups On Campus. But even though it is well known, many people still wonder: "What do they actually do?" "What benefits does joining a club like this bring to my learning and future?"

As a current GDG on Campus Lead at National Taiwan University of Science and Technology, I can use this article to give everyone a closer look.

# What Is GDG on Campus?
GDG on Campus was formerly known as Google Developer Student Clubs. Its core idea is to help students get exposure to the latest technology tools and trends through an open, collaborative community, so they can improve their technical abilities and even find like-minded partners to build innovative projects together.

Its main activities generally include:
There may be slight differences depending on each school's community, but most follow a setup similar to the following:

**1. Regular Weekly Sessions**

On one fixed day each week, core team members or senior students are invited to teach specific technical topics such as ML/DL, Cloud Computing, Frontend, Backend, and more, so you can directly engage with the latest technologies and get hands-on practice.

**2. Expert Talks and Technical Workshops**

Each semester, we hold expert talks where well-known people from industry share their work experiences and AI application technologies, allowing you to directly connect with professionals, tools, technologies, and ideas.

**3. Project Development and Competitions**

The club encourages members to form teams and participate in various technical challenges, such as the Google Solution Challenge or local hackathons, to build real-world experience.

**4. Community Interaction and Growth**

We also organize informal social activities such as Coffee Chats and company visits. At NTUST, for example, we have visited companies like LINE and Google. These activities help members build strong connections, learn how to grow within a team, and broaden their horizons.

# What Are the Benefits of Joining GDG on Campus?
**1. Access to the Latest Technologies and Resources**

You will have opportunities to access official Google resources, such as free chances to take technical certification exams or participate in the annual Cloud Study Jam. In 2024, more than five people in our community received the highest-level rewards, including Google backpacks, water bottles, and stickers.

After stepping down this year, I even received a free one-year developer program membership worth USD 299.

![Benefit](../../../assets/posts/google-developer-groups-on-campus-apply/Google%20Benefit.png)

**2. Expand Your Network and Connect to the Future**

Whether in academia, industry, or among members from other schools, you can meet people from different backgrounds here, and even Google employees.

**3. Practice Soft Skills**

Beyond technical skills, joining the club also helps you develop leadership, communication, and event planning abilities, all of which are valuable for future job hunting.

## How Do I Apply to Become a GDG on Campus Member?
Please follow your own school's GDG on Campus community. Most of them currently have Instagram pages, and you can directly send them a message to ask.

NTUST: [@gdg.ntust](https://www.instagram.com/gdg.ntust/)

## How Do I Apply to Join the GDG on Campus Core Team?
Core Team recruitment is managed by each club's Lead, so please message the community's social media account directly. Usually, we recruit new officers every academic year, and application forms open during winter or summer break. Remember to prepare your basic information, interests, and a brief introduction to your skills, and also make sure to message us first.

## How Do I Apply to Become a GDG on Campus Lead?
This is probably the part everyone is most interested in. Becoming a GDG on Campus Lead not only gives you influence within a technical community, but also helps you improve your organizational abilities, expand your network, and even gain opportunities to interact directly with Google. If you are interested in becoming a Lead, here is the full application and preparation process:

### Step 1: Make Sure You Meet the Basic Qualifications
The selection process for GDG on Campus Leads places some emphasis on both technical background and leadership potential. Usually, the following areas are considered:

* Technical ability: Have basic development experience. Familiarity with Google technologies such as Firebase, TensorFlow, Flutter, and so on is a plus.
* Teamwork experience: Have you participated in technical projects or related club activities before?
* Passion and influence: Are you passionate about leading a technical community? Do you have the ability to energize and organize activities?

### Step 2: Submit the Application Form
Google currently uses a nomination-based system, so be sure to ask the current Lead at your school first. Applications for GDG on Campus Lead usually open around April to May each year.

The application form will usually ask for the following:

* Basic personal information such as your name, school, department, and contact information.
* Technical background and experience, such as projects you have participated in and the technologies you have used.
* Your vision and plans for GDG on Campus, including how you hope to lead the community.

### Step 3: Attend the Interview
After submitting the form, Google's Relationship Manager will invite you to a one-on-one interview, conducted fully in Chinese, to evaluate the following:

* Technical depth and learning ability: How do you quickly learn new technologies and apply them to community activities?
* Leadership and coordination ability: Can you organize events effectively and coordinate collaboration among members?
* Innovative thinking: Can you bring unique activities and value to the community?

There is no need to be overly nervous about this part. As long as you sincerely share your experiences and passion, that is enough. You can also use this chance to ask any questions you have.

### Step 4: Attend Training and Receive Your Official Appointment
Once selected, you will participate in a Lead Onboarding Event organized by Google. It usually lasts about a full day, and the content includes:

* Google technology and resource training
* Practical community operations
* How to build and maintain community culture

After completing the training, you officially become a GDG on Campus Lead and begin leading your campus community.

# What Are the Benefits of Becoming a Lead?
* Support from official Google resources: You will receive Google technical resources and badge support. Direct funding opportunities are relatively rare.
* Career development opportunities: If you can lead the club to produce medium- to large-scale projects during your term, that becomes a meaningful highlight that makes you more competitive.
* Personal growth and influence: As a Lead, you will learn how to organize events, manage teams, and become a technical leader on campus. You will also meet many extremely skilled people from your own school and others ⚡.
