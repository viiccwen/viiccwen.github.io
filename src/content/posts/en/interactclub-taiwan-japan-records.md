# Preface

In June 2023, an uncle asked whether I would be willing to join the Taiwan-Japan Interact Club exchange event organized by the Rotary Club of Keelung East. From 2023/7/24 to 2023/7/27, I would be responsible for welcoming and accompanying the senior Rotary members from the Yatsushiro South Rotary Club in Japan and the students from the Yatsushiro Interact Club who had traveled a long way to come here. Together, we would take part in friendship-building, sports, and public service exchanges, including beach cleaning and helping clean the homes of elderly people living alone. I thought about it for a few days and felt it would be a really good experience, so I decided to join. I even turned down an invitation to the Ruizhong AID summer program afterward QQ.

# Day 1

The schedule for the first day was: **airport pickup at Taoyuan Airport, Heping Island, and Keelung Miaokou Night Market.**

On the first day (2023/7/24), Xiaochuang and I went to Taoyuan Airport with the uncles and aunties from the Rotary Clubs of Keelung East and New Taipei Ruifang to pick up the Japanese group XD. It just happened to be the day of the Wan An air raid drill too, so we had to leave an hour early to avoid getting stuck on the road.

While waiting, one of the aunties even bought us Starbucks. I drank a chocolate java chip frappuccino (a large cost 160 NTD...), and Xiaochuang and I both almost felt sick from drinking so much XD. He got an XL... At the same time, we were flipping through everyone's photos and names, looking forward to meeting them. I also asked Xiaochuang some Japanese, since I only knew a few phrases.

After we picked them up, I realized... why were they all girls XD. And they were all pretty shy too, which made the first day full of awkwardness. Even though we tried to close the distance with them, the power of just two grown men was really too limited... (everyone else, come help us already aaaaaah!).

After the pickup, we headed to Heping Island to walk around and chat. We even went down to soak our feet for a bit, though having wet feet was such a hassle. We also took photos with the Japanese students, got to know one another, and memorized each other's names: Minami, Nana, Miyu, Mao, Mei, and Ai. Everyone had their own distinct personality, which I will mention later.

Our schedule for the second day was: **National Taiwan Ocean University Education Expo, National Museum of Marine Science and Technology, badminton matches, Taiwan-Japan Rotary regular meeting, and singing at Hyatt KTV.**

In the morning, we met at the NTOU Education Expo, and other group members finally arrived. Our group got three more people, and Heng Yi also brought three students. We introduced ourselves to each other, got acquainted, and then moved into the very boring big group photo session... They reallyyyy love taking pictures.

After lunch, we went to Ruigong to play badminton, and this turned out to be a real turning point in our relationships.

We split into groups and played badminton, which sounds pretty normal, but they were all fully geared up XD. Badminton shoes, sportswear, everything. They were incredibly professional about it. You could really see how seriously they took every part of the schedule. Compared with the casual laziness of Taiwanese people, they were honestly super serious hahaha.

Among them, Mao and Nana felt like school-team badminton players. Their movements were very standard, and they were really good. Big thumbs-up to them.

Miyu may watch too much anime, because she kept making "ah!" and "ya!" sounds while playing, which was iconic. And thanks to her reactions, everyone's relationships got much closer XD.

Overall, everyone was really serious, which left a great impression. They did not feel difficult to get along with or careless at all. If anything, we were the ones being more casual, and I was honestly worried they might dislike us hahaha.

At the end, I drove Xiaochuang home. A typhoon had happened to arrive around those days, and the wind was so strong and the rain was absolutely brutal. Riding my little scooter felt like being a tiny boat in front of a giant wave, like I could capsize at any moment XD. So I just embraced the absurdity and rode through the rain. As for what time I got home... once again, it was after 11. Enough said. Shower and sleep. Tomorrow was the last day.

# Day 3

The third day's schedule was: **community service (cleaning the homes of elderly people living alone), beach cleanup (changed to the Gold Museum), and the Taiwan-Japan Interact Club reflection-sharing and farewell event.**

This was the day when the typhoon hit northern Taiwan the hardest. It had been raining on and off since morning, and the wind was also very strong, so the original beach cleanup activity had to be changed to a trip to the Gold Museum. That was a bit of a pity.

In the morning, we reached an agreement with the village chief of a local neighborhood and went to the homes of elderly people living alone to help clean for them. The first grandma was already 92 years old. Her house had exactly that "grandma's house" feeling, with lots of things that looked like they had not been touched in years, but she still said she needed them. There was even a melon inside that had already been eaten rotten by ants, and she still had not thrown it away... so we helped her throw it out. There was also a Coke bottle lying on the floor that had probably been there for ages, and she said her daughter still needed it... seriously?＿？Anyway, we just kept cleaning and cleaning, chatting with the people next to us while we worked, and it was actually pretty enjoyable. The grandma also talked with us and seemed very healthy.

After that, we were taken to some hotel and rented a room for an hour so we could change clothes and rest for a bit. I remember thinking: this is unbelievable... they really cared that much about how we felt QQ. I almost wanted to cry.

So of course I showed proper appreciation. Since we had the room anyway, I just took a shower.

When the time came, we headed to the Evergreen Laurel Hotel to prepare for the reflection-sharing event and farewell gathering. Our Japanese friends were leaving the next day, sob sob QQ.

After that, it was a lot of eating and chatting. Xiaochuang shared some reflections, and I thought one thing he said was really good: "Over these three days, we went to so many places. I will never forget those memories, but what I will never forget most is you, our Japanese friends." (Something along those lines.) Honestly, hearing it gave me goosebumps. That was such a killer line, bro.

Later, I got dragged on stage to sing too. Everyone was so excited and so happy, whether they were adults or Interact Club students, whether they were Taiwanese or our friends from Japan. At that moment, everyone was smiling so brightly. It is truly a scene I will never forget for the rest of my life QQ. I am really glad I got to join this event.

When it was finally time to leave, nobody wanted to part. Everyone kept taking photos and talking. Even when the tour bus had already arrived, nobody wanted to go. In the end, we even got on the bus with them and accompanied them back to their hotel, where we kept chatting outside for quite a while.

# Closing Thoughts

During this time, I saw many differences between Taiwan and Japan. They are extremely unwilling to trouble other people. Even if the hotel air conditioning could not be adjusted and it was too hot, they would not complain to us. Only when I asked would they explain it simply. If they had been Taiwanese, they probably would have been complaining nonstop already hahaha.

They also tend to do everything as perfectly as possible. Even for something as simple as a badminton match, they gave it their all and showed up fully equipped, which really surprised us.

I also met many people through this event, whether from the Heng Yi High School Interact Club or from the Yatsushiro South Interact Club in Japan. All of it became a beautiful memory. At first everyone was a bit shy and reserved, and it was hard to know how to get to know each other. Because of the language barrier, we could not communicate normally either. But people always find a way out. Through English plus sign language, we managed to communicate successfully XD, which was honestly very moving. We were basically diplomacy ambassadors x.

Even though these few days were exhausting and a typhoon hit us, none of it could stop our enthusiasm.

May the friendship between Taiwan and Japan last forever. Let this post serve as a recorded gift dedicated to everyone.
